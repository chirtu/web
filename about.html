15+ Years of Excellence

Connecting Tanzania's Education & Research Institutions

TERNET is a network of Tanzania higher education and research institutions aiming at providing network infrastructure and associated services for enabling sharing of education and research resources inside and outside the country.

Founded in 2008

Registered as a Trust on 11th April 2008

UbuntuNet Alliance Member

Joined on 18th April 2008

International Connectivity

Peers with GEANT through UbuntuNet Alliance

TERNET structure consists of five organs: Board of Trustees comprised of maximum of 10 key education and research stakeholders, Executive Committee comprised of maximum of 10 deputies of heads of member institutions, and the Secretariat headed by the Executive Secretary. At the top of all TERNET operations there is an Annual General Meeting (AGM) comprising of all heads of member institutions.

Foreword from the Chief Executive Officer

Dear Partners, Members, and Stakeholders,

It is my honor and privilege to lead TERNET as we continue our journey towards transforming higher education and research connectivity in Tanzania and beyond. Over the years, we have made significant strides in providing reliable, high-speed network infrastructure that enables our member institutions to access global educational resources and collaborate on groundbreaking research.

Our commitment to excellence remains unwavering. We continue to invest in cutting-edge technology, build strategic partnerships, and nurture talent to ensure that TERNET remains the preferred network for Higher Education and Research Institutions (HERIs) in Tanzania. Our Foundation

Guiding Principles

VISION

To be a world leading education and research network through reliable, sustainable, and affordable use of ICTs.

MISSION

1. To provide affordable and reliable broadband connectivity to member institutions and be the preferred network for HERIs.

2. To enable education and research institutions to exploit their full potential using ICTs through collective bargaining and sharing of resources.

OBJECTIVES

The following are the objectives for the establishment of TERNET

1

To establish a sustainable and high capacity network infrastructure connecting educational and research institutions in Tanzania

2

To increase the usage of the computer as a tool that stimulates the creativity, critical analysis, the divergent thoughts, the expressive capacity and problems resolution.

3

To enable Tanzania students to construct their own knowledge as they learn and to develop skills necessary in the highly technological modern society

4

To establish a human resource base of highly qualified people that will increase the country's international competence and its successful participation in the globalization process.

5

To support the establishment and sharing of teaching and learning resources among educational and research institutions in and outside Tanzania.

6

To enable linkages with and among various economic sectors that can leverage national development.

CORE VALUES

In order to ensure TERNET sustainability, TERNET (members and the secretariat) is committing to the following 10 core values.

Commitment

TERNET is committed to fulfill its objectives

Reliability

TERNET is committed to maintain the highest standards in all its operations

Integrity

TERNET is committed to conduct its business in accordance with the highest standard of professional behavior and ethics

Fairness

TERNET is committed to be fair in all its dealing with members

Partnership

TERNET is committed to nurture partnerships with its members

Accountability

TERNET is committed to accept responsibility for all its actions

Sustainability

TERNET is committed to be self-sustaining in all its operations

Competitiveness

TERNET is committed to offer competitive services to its stakeholders

Open Access

TERNET is committed to openly share opinions and views

Innovation

TERNET is committed to always be at the forefront of innovation for the benefit and sustainability of its members.
